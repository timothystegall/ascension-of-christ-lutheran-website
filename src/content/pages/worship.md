    <h2 class="text-accent-blue uppercase text-[1.2rem] tracking-[2px] mt-0">Closed Communion</h2>
    <p class="mt-[0.25rem]">Due to our confession of the Eucharist being the true Body and Blood of Christ, we practice the historic discipline of Closed Communion. Visitors who desire to commune are kindly asked to speak with the Pastor before the service.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="border-l-[3px] border-accent bg-[rgba(255,255,255,0.02)] py-[25px] px-[40px] mb-[40px]">
    <h2 class="text-accent-blue uppercase text-[1.2rem] tracking-[2px] mt-0">Choir & Handbell Program</h2>
    <p class="mt-[0.25rem]">Music at Ascension of Christ is not entertainment, but a vehicle for the Word. We invite members to participate in the choral and instrumental life of the parish:</p>
    <ul class="list-none p-0">
      <li class="mb-[10px]">• <strong class="text-accent">Parish Choir:</strong> Occassionally leads the congregation in the Propers and Ordinary, as well as historic hymns and anthems.</li>
      <li>• <strong class="text-accent">Handbell Choir:</strong> Adds festive solemnity to our major feasts and festival services throughout the Church Year.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="border-l-[3px] border-accent bg-[rgba(255,255,255,0.02)] py-[25px] px-[40px] mb-[40px]">
    <h2 class="text-accent-blue uppercase text-[1.2rem] tracking-[2px] mt-0">Understanding the Liturgy: The Divine Service</h2>
    <p class="mt-[0.25rem]">We primarily utilize <strong class="text-accent">Setting Three</strong> from the <em>Lutheran Service Book (LSB)</em>. Based on the historic "Common Service," which itself has its roots in the historic liturgy of the Western Church, it is a choral dialogue between the Pastor and the Congregation.</p>
    
    <div class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5 text-[0.95rem]">
      <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Introit:</strong> We enter into God's presence with Psalms, usually chanted by the Pastor, or sometimes the choir.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Kyrie:</strong> "Lord, have mercy." We cry out to Christ.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Gloria in Excelsis:</strong> The song of the angels at Christ's birth, celebrating God's favor toward man.</p>
      </div>
      <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Sanctus:</strong> "Holy, Holy, Holy." We join our voices with the angels and the whole company of heaven.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Agnus Dei:</strong> "Lamb of God." We prepare to receive the Body and Blood of Him who takes away the sin of the world.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Nunc Dimittis:</strong> "Lord, now lettest Thou Thy servant depart in peace," having seen and received our Salvation.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="border-l-[3px] border-accent bg-[rgba(255,255,255,0.02)] py-[25px] px-[40px] mb-[40px]">
    <h2 class="text-accent-blue uppercase text-[1.2rem] tracking-[2px] mt-0">The Order of the Divine Service: Setting Three</h2>
    <p class="mt-[0.25rem]">We primarily utilize <strong class="text-accent">Setting Three</strong> from the <em>Lutheran Service Book (LSB)</em>. Based on the historic "Common Service," which itself has its roots in the historic liturgy of the Western Church. It is a dialogue between God and His people, where He gives and we receive.</p>

  <h3 class="text-light text-[1rem] uppercase mt-[30px] border-b border-border-dim pb-1">I. Preparation</h3>
  <div class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5 text-[0.95rem]">
    <div>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">Confession & Absolution:</strong> We begin by kneeling to confess our sins. The Pastor, acting as a called & ordained servant in the stead and by the command of Christ, pronounces the forgiveness of all our sins.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Introit:</strong> The "Entrance" Psalm. It sets the theological tone for the specific Sunday in the Church Year.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <h3 class="text-light text-[1rem] uppercase mt-[30px] border-b border-border-dim pb-1">II. Service of the Word</h3>
  <div class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5 text-[0.95rem]">
    <div>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Kyrie:</strong> "Lord, have mercy." The prayer of the beggar before the King, asking for help in all our needs.</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Gloria in Excelsis:</strong> The Great Gloria. We join the angels of Bethlehem in praising the Triune God.</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Salutation & Collect:</strong> "The Lord be with you." The Pastor and People greet one another before the specific "Prayer of the Day" (the Collect) is offered.</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Readings:</strong> Usually an Old Testament lesson, an Epistle, and the Holy Gospel. We stand for the Gospel as a sign of reverence for Christ's words.</p>
    </div>
    <div>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Alleluia & Verse:</strong> A short scriptural song of anticipation before the Gospel reading.</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Creed:</strong> Usually the Nicene Creed, where we confess the "one holy catholic and apostolic Church."</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Hymn of the Day:</strong> The chief hymn of the service that reinforces the Gospel message of the day.</p>
      <p><strong class="text-accent">The Sermon:</strong> The living voice of the Gospel, where the Law is applied to our hearts and the Gospel is delivered for our comfort.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <h3 class="text-light text-[1rem] uppercase mt-[30px] border-b border-border-dim pb-1">III. Service of the Sacrament</h3>
    <div class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5 text-[0.95rem]">
        <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Offertory:</strong> "Create in me a clean heart, O God." We prepare for the Holy Supper.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Preface:</strong> The ancient dialogue ("Lift up your hearts") leading into the administration of the Eucharist.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Sanctus:</strong> "Holy, Holy, Holy." We join the Seraphim and the palm-branch-waving crowds of Jerusalem ("Hosanna!").</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Lord's Prayer:</strong> The prayer Christ taught us, spoken or chanted before the elements are consecrated.</p>
        </div>
        <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Words of Institution:</strong> The Pastor speaks Christ's own words over the bread and wine. We believe these words do what they say: give us Christ's Body and Blood.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Pax Domini:</strong> "The Peace of the Lord be with you always."</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Agnus Dei:</strong> "Lamb of God." We sing to Christ who is now truly present on the Altar.</p>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Distribution:</strong> The faithful come forward to the rail to eat and drink for the forgiveness of sins.</p>
        </div>
    </div>

    <h3 class="text-accent text-[1rem] uppercase mt-[30px] border-b border-border-dim pb-1">IV. Post-Communion & Dismissal</h3>
    <div class="grid grid-cols-1 sm:grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5 text-[0.95rem]">
        <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Nunc Dimittis:</strong> The Song of Simeon. Like him, we have seen and tasted our Salvation, and can now "depart in peace."</p>
        </div>
        <div>
        <p><strong class="text-accent">The Benediction:</strong> The Aaronic Blessing. The Pastor places God's name upon the people as they go back into the world to serve their neighbor.</p>
        </div>
    </div>
